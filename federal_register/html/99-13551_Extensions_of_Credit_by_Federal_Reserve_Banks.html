Extensions of Credit by Federal Reserve Banks

Type: Proposed Rule | Citation: 64 FR 28768

Published: 1999-05-27

Document Number: 99-13551

Action

Proposed rule.

Abstract

The Board is proposing to amend its Regulation A to establish a special lending program under which Federal Reserve Banks will extend credit at a rate above the Federal Open Market Committee's targeted federal funds rate to eligible institutions to accommodate liquidity needs during the century date change period. Unlike adjustment credit, borrowers would not be required to seek credit elsewhere first, uses of funds would not be limited, and the loans could be outstanding for a considerable period.
