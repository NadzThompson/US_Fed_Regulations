# Securities Credit Transactions; Borrowing by Brokers and Dealers

**Proposed Rule** | 61 FR 60168 | Published 1996-11-26

**Document Number:** 96-30003

---

## Action

Proposed rule.

## Abstract

On October 11, 1996, the President signed the National Securities Markets Improvement Act of 1996 (the Markets Improvement Act). Under the Markets Improvement Act, the Board no longer has the authority to regulate certain loans to registered broker-dealers unless it finds that such rules are necessary or appropriate in the public interest or for the protection of investors. The Markets Improvement Act also repeals section 8(a) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 (the Exchange Act), which limited the sources of credit for broker- dealers who pledge exchange-traded equity securities to certain banks and other broker-dealers. The Board is soliciting comment on amendments to its margin regulations (Regulations G, T and U) to implement the statutory amendments in the Markets Improvement Act and further the policies behind their adoption.
