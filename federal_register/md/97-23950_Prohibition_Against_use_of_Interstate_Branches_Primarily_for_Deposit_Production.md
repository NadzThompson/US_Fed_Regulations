# Prohibition Against use of Interstate Branches Primarily for Deposit Production

**Final Rule** | 62 FR 47728 | Published 1997-09-10
**Effective:** 1997-10-10
**Document Number:** 97-23950

---

## Action

Joint final rule.

## Abstract

The OCC, Board, and FDIC (collectively, agencies) are adopting uniform regulations to implement section 109 (section 109) of the Riegle-Neal Interstate Banking and Branching Efficiency Act of 1994 (Interstate Act). The final rule reflects comments received on the proposal and further internal consideration by the agencies. As required by section 109, the final rule prohibits any bank from establishing or acquiring a branch or branches outside of its home state under the Interstate Act primarily for the purpose of deposit production, and provides guidelines for determining whether such bank is reasonably helping to meet the credit needs of the communities served by these branches.
